# LOCATION HISTORY SCHEMA

Location records document the places, environments, houses, schools, neighborhoods, workplaces, cities, and recurring settings that shaped the user's life.

Locations are important timeline anchors. They should be documented carefully and linked to incidents, eras, people, and routines.

---

# Metadata

- Place ID:
- Place Name:
- Place Type:
- Date Range:
- Certainty:
- Address or Area:
- City or Region:
- Source:
- Related Eras:
- Related Incidents:
- Related People:
- Tags:

---

# Location Overview

Short factual description of the place and its role in the user's life.

Do not invent sensory or architectural details.

---

# Timeline Use

Record how this place helps anchor chronology:
- first known connection
- last known connection
- associated school, job, family phase, relationship, device, or life era
- events that confirm or narrow the date range

---

# Environment

Document only sourced details:
- rooms
- street or neighborhood
- routines
- sounds
- objects
- travel routes
- social environment
- safety or instability
- economic context
- digital context connected to the location

Use uncertainty labels when memory is partial.

---

# Associated People

List people connected to this place and their role.

---

# Associated Incidents

Link incidents that happened here or were shaped by this place.

---

# Emotional Meaning

Document what the place meant to the user, based on source material.

Separate:
- remembered facts
- emotional interpretation at the time
- emotional interpretation now
- unresolved uncertainty

---

# Open Questions

List questions needed to clarify the place, timeline, people, or emotional meaning.
