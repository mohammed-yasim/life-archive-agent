## Full Narrative

The broader firm/group was OPTO, with business names or functions including OPTONET, OPTOSOFT, OPTOSYSTEMS, and OPTO TRADE LINKS. The user describes OPTONET as the internet cafe / computer center side. OPTO was located at Kanjirapally, while Arshad's home was at Edakkunnam.

Arshad was not a distant stranger. He lived in the same area, about 1 km away, and shared the same community/masjid context. Before the invitation, the user had already visited OPTO multiple times for ordinary service needs provided by the firm, such as printing, internet, applications, service, school work, and similar needs.

Arshad invited the user into the OPTO space. There was no formal role or title. The user helped Arshad when needed and used the infrastructure for tech exploration. During vacations, holidays, and weekends, the user sometimes went for more dedicated help or work for him.

During the Govt THS years, OPTO became parallel to school life. After class ended around 3:30-4:00 PM, the user often went from Govt THS Koovappally to OPTO Kanjirapally, then returned home to Edakkunnam. Leaving time depended on the day and need: buses around 4:50, 5:05, 5:30, 6:15, 7:25 PM, or sometimes travelling with Arshad.

Arshad's role in the user's learning was both direct and indirect. He explained what he knew, referred or suggested things, and also provided access and trust. The user does not currently remember the first specific tech explorations after the invitation, but identifies the major shift as "a lot diving into tech."

Father objected to the OPTO/Arshad connection because of political or ideological differences between father and Arshad, not because of a personal conflict. The objection became active sometimes when father was drunk.
