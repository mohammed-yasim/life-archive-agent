# LIFE ERAS SCHEMA

Life era records group periods of the user's life into meaningful phases.

An era is not just a date range. It is a documented environment with recurring people, places, routines, pressures, transitions, and emotional themes.

---

- Era ID:
- Era Title:
- Date Range:

- Source:
- Tags:

---

# Era Summary

Brief factual overview of the period.

Include only known or clearly marked approximate information.

---

# Timeline Anchors

List events that define or anchor the era:
- moves
- school changes
- jobs
- relationships
- family events
- health events
- digital-life changes
- major incidents

Each anchor should include a certainty label.

---

# Environment

Document the user's surroundings:
- home
- school
- workplace
- neighborhood
- social environment
- online environment
- daily routine
- economic context
- cultural context

Do not invent sensory detail.

---

# People and Relationships

List important people in this era and link relationship records where available.

For each person:
- role
- period of relevance
- known impact
- unresolved questions

---

# Emotional and Identity Themes

Record emotional and identity development based on user account and evidence.

Keep themes grounded in specific events.

Avoid diagnostic language.

---

# Transitions

Document:
- what led into the era
- what ended the era
- what changed afterward
- what carried forward

---

# Gaps and Open Questions

List missing years, unclear transitions, uncertain people, unresolved incidents, and evidence needed.
